# Bounds

**Position bounds** clamp the transform’s translation: `positionX` and `positionY`
stay within the ranges you set. They apply while panning, during pinch or wheel
zoom, and when inertia or animations run—anything that would move the content
past a limit is stopped or corrected at that edge.

Use these props when you need a **custom panning window** (for example a large
map or canvas where you do not want users to drag content completely away, or
where min/max should **not** match the wrapper edges). For the common case
“keep the scaled content inside the viewport,” use **`limitToBounds`** (see the
controls below): the library derives limits from the wrapper, content size, and
current scale. When you pass a given `minPosition*` / `maxPosition*` value, it
**replaces** the auto-calculated bound on that axis; this story sets all four,
so the pan range is defined entirely by those numbers.

## What this story shows

The viewport is a fixed **500×500px** frame. The map (`BoundsContent` in
`bounds.data.tsx`) is much larger, so you can pan in both directions. The
min/max values are chosen so you can explore the map but **cannot** pan past
the configured limits—try dragging toward each edge until motion stops.

The **fog and “Limit” labels** are implemented by `BoundsOverlay` in the
story utilities (`useTransformComponent` + edge proximity). They are **not**
part of the published library; they only illustrate how you might reflect
“near a bound” in your own UI.
